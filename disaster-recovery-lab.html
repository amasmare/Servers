Disaster Recovery Sites & RTO/RPO

Understand the three types of DR sites and use the sliders to see how RTO/RPO requirements map to site choices.

🔥 Hot Site
Fully operational mirror of production. Real-time data replication. Failover in minutes.

RTO: Minutes
RPO: Near-zero
Cost: Highest
Use: Banks, hospitals, stock exchanges
🌡️ Warm Site
Hardware is installed and ready but data must be restored. Failover in hours.

RTO: Hours
RPO: Hours to 1 day
Cost: Medium
Use: Most enterprises
❄️ Cold Site
Physical space and power only. Must procure and configure all equipment after disaster.

RTO: Days to weeks
RPO: Days
Cost: Lowest
Use: Non-critical systems

📖 Key DR Terminology

RTO
Recovery Time Objective — maximum acceptable time for systems to be restored after a disaster.
RPO
Recovery Point Objective — maximum acceptable data loss measured in time (e.g., 4-hour RPO = restore from backup max 4 hours old).
BCP
Business Continuity Plan — comprehensive plan to keep business operations running during/after disaster.
DRP
Disaster Recovery Plan — technical plan for restoring IT systems specifically. Subset of BCP.
MTTR
Mean Time To Repair — average time to restore a failed component or system.
MTBF
Mean Time Between Failures — average time between hardware failures. Measure of reliability.
